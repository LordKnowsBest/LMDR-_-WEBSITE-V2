## Why Allure Matters (Investor Narrative)

### 1. Proves Repeat Demand
Allure didn't use VelocityMatch once and leave. They've come back **31 times over 13 months**. This isn't a one-time transaction — it's an ongoing operational dependency. They've made VelocityMatch part of how they hire.

### 2. Validates the Core Problem
Allure is a well-run carrier — family-owned, 35 years in business, brand-new equipment, competitive pay (60¢/mile). They're not a bottom-feeder struggling to attract drivers because of poor conditions. Even **good carriers** can't fill seats fast enough. The problem is structural, not situational.

### 3. Demonstrates Price Acceptance
At $1,200 per placement, Allure has paid nearly $18K without pushing back on pricing. For context:
- Traditional staffing agencies charge 20-30% of first-year salary ($13K-$24K per hire)
- VelocityMatch at $1,200 is a **90% cost reduction** vs. traditional agencies
- The ROI is obvious: $1,200 to fill a seat that generates $65K-$80K in revenue capacity

### 4. Shows Stickiness Over Time
13 consecutive months of engagement means:
- Allure's hiring need is ongoing (not a one-time surge)
- They're satisfied enough to keep coming back (not shopping alternatives)
- The relationship has survived long enough to prove durability

### 5. Creates the Upsell Path
Allure is the ideal first software customer because:
- They already trust VelocityMatch
- They have ongoing compliance needs (DQ files, medical cards, MVRs for every driver placed)
- A $500-$2,000/month compliance dashboard is a natural extension of the relationship
- They'd likely provide a testimonial or reference for investor conversations

## What the Platform Captures From Every Allure Placement

This is the data flywheel in action. Every placement generates structured data across:

| Data Layer | What's Captured |
|---|---|
| **Driver Profile** | Experience, endorsements, location, preferences, career history |
| **Job Match** | Match score, preference alignment, deal-breaker detection |
| **Funnel Analytics** | Source channel, time-to-apply, time-to-hire, drop-off points |
| **Outreach Effectiveness** | Which messages got responses, which channels converted |
| **Compliance Docs** | CDL status, medical card expiry, drug screen results, MVR |
| **Placement Outcome** | Hired Y/N, start date, onboarding completion |
| **Retention Signal** | 30/60/90-day retention status (when trackable) |

Over 31 transactions across 13 months, the Allure relationship alone has generated hundreds of data points that train the matching algorithm.
